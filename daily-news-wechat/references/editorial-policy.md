# Editorial policy

## Time and eligibility

- Default window: Beijing time `[previous day 06:00, current day 06:00)`.
- Use event or official-announcement time for editorial eligibility; retain publication time for traceability.
- Unknown or ambiguous time means review, never automatic selection.

## Source policy

- Tier 1: government departments, regulators, local authorities, official emergency channels.
- Tier 2: national authoritative media.
- Tier 3: established business, technology, and general newsrooms.
- Tier 4: aggregators, blogs, social posts, and unknown sources; use only as leads.
- Policy, disaster, and public-safety stories require a Tier 1 source.

## Selection

- Score importance, reach, timeliness, reliability, and ordinary-person relevance.
- Merge reports about the same event. Prefer the best primary source and keep additional sources in verification notes.
- Default final count: 5–8; at least four categories; at most two stories per category.
- Never fill quotas with old news, promotional copy, rumors, or weakly related items.

## Writing

- Structure each item as: what happened, why it matters, what ordinary people should notice, and a short actionable reminder.
- Separate verified facts from analysis. Avoid sensational titles and unsupported causal claims.
- Mark dynamic numbers with an explicit cutoff time.
- Put sources at the end as linked article titles; do not show bare URLs.

## Failure behavior

- If fewer than five qualified items or fewer than four categories remain, mark `needs_review`.
- If important figures conflict, record both sources and omit the disputed number from final copy.
- If a critical source fails, do not substitute an unverified claim.
